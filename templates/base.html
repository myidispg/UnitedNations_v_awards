about unv

The United Nations Volunteers (UNV) programme contributes to peace and development through volunteerism worldwide. We work with partners to integrate qualified, highly motivated and well supported UN Volunteers into development programming and promote the value and global recognition of volunteerism. UNV is active in around 130 countries every year. With Field Presences in over 80 countries, UNV is represented worldwide. UNV is administered by the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) and reports to the UNDP Executive Board.

UNV awards

Volunteerism is an important, and increasingly popular, mechanism for young people to bring about positive change in society, and it is becoming more and more relevant as a mechanism to engage young people in human development. Volunteers are constantly contributing and positively influencing India’s social development. It has been highlighted that promoting volunteerism by rewarding and recognizing volunteer’s efforts towards creating a conducive environment is looked as one of the crucial elements towards encouraging people to volunteer. Efforts have been promoted both by Government agencies and private institution to channelize the effort of volunteering by celebrating, rewarding and promoting volunteerism and volunteers in India. This framework would help a host coordinating agency or a consortium to take forward institution of an award and its implementation.
There is a felt need to promote culture of youth volunteering in the country and therefore it’s all the more important to reward young people inspiring social change. Long term goal is to: Motivate100 young persons (aged between 18 – 29 years) per annum to achieve excellence in the field of national development or social service in the long term. In this process also inspire incubation of state- level awards with at least 10 states creating a platform for celebrating 10 young leaders each. Encourage young people to develop a sense of responsibility to the community and thus to improve their own personal potential as active citizens Create a culture of youth volunteering in the country through rewarding young leaders inspiring social change As a pilot it is proposed to institute one national award with 12 awardees to begin with but the long-term vision is that all states adopt this model and run state wise programmes too. The long-term goal is to have a cohort of 100 awardees nationally eventually with states having 10 awardees each.
